About

Our hackathon's theme this year is Ed-Tech, which is a field of technology centered around enhancing students' learning experiences both inside and outside the classroom. Our Ed-Tech Hackathon will run from March 31 - April 2, 2023.

Join us for a fun-filled weekend of coding, designing, and brainstorming ideas to develop solutions that have the potential to transform education for the better. We've got workshops, alumni panel and icebreaker activities planned to keep you energized and engaged throughout the event.

There will alse be over $2000 in prizes for category winners!

We can't wait to see what you will create!

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

Put simply, a hackathon is a chance to build whatever you want. Have an idea? The Dons Hack is the place to make it a reality. A hackathon gives you the opportunity to build your portfolio, develop skills, and learn how to work in a team.

Who can participate?

All USFCA students can participate regardless of major or gender. No prior coding or technical experience is necessary!

Is The Dons Hack fully in-person this year?

Yes! The Dons Hack will be in-person this year! It will be located on campus (check the schedule for more specific locations). Workspace rooms will also be available overnight.

How much does it cost?

The Dons Hack is free for our student attendees! We will provide meals for the entire duration of the event.

Do I need to have a team to apply? Is there a limit to the size of each team?

It is not necessary to have a team at the time of registration. We will help you form a team. If you choose to build a team, there can be a maximum of 4 people.

What should I bring?

Some essential things to bring include a laptop (with charger), a change of clothes if you plan on spending the night, and a sleeping bag/blanket if you’re not local.
